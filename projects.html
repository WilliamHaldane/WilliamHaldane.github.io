Computer Science Projects

Summer '23 Internship

One of the first things I did as an intern over the summer was implement a variety of changes to my companies Azure account. Mainly, I was tasked with configuring the IT team's Devops environment, using Azure Repos, Boards and also establishing several pipelines from previous repositories to our new repository in Repos. I also collected and centralized all of our current and backed up tasks, creating a one stop shop for us and the executives to be able to track reliably. I also studied the different development team strategies and picked the one I thought would work best for our team, agile.

A bulk of my experience was working with many different Microsoft Apps. I worked with Power Apps, Power Pages, Power BI, and more. Power Pages is a fairly new Microsoft service that allows you to easily get the basic layout of a website, skipping much of the work needed for repetative html or css. It also discards the need to worry about hosting the website as it is all run, charged and secured through your Microsoft account. I worked with our full time Software Developer and another intern to bring forth the basic layout of our website using Power Pages, and then created many different customizations, methods and features using CSS, html and Javascript. As I finished the summer, the website was not completely finished, but I was able to start the process of learning html, Css and Javascript and gained valuable insight on how working on a development team works.

Within Power Apps, I configured several different Apps for my company. The most extensive was one to help with asset management. Mainly focusing on hardware tracking, I created, modified and customized a Power App that could be launched on mobile devices or via laptops/desktops. I added many custom features and tools to better help my organization track their assets and then recorded my data. See Figure 1 for more.

I was also able to use Power Automate. Our data scientist was doing several different weekly reports, many of which were very labor intensive, mistake prone and repetative. With a combination of Excel scripts and Power Automate flows, I was able to automate 90% of the work she was doing on these reports, freeing up her time to do more thought provoking and interesting work.
